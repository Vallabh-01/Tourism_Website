Champa Shashthi

Description:

Champa Shashthi is a significant festival in Maharashtra dedicated to Lord Khandoba, a revered deity in the region. It marks the end of the six-day worship period and is celebrated with great fervor, especially in the Jejuri and Pali regions.

Significance:

The festival signifies the victory of good over evil, commemorating Lord Khandoba's triumph over the demons Mani and Malla. It is a time for devotees to seek blessings and protection from the deity.

Customs and Rituals:

Devotees perform special pujas, offer turmeric, and light lamps. The six-day celebration includes processions, music, and dances, culminating in grand festivities on Champa Shashthi.

Special Foods:

Offerings of various traditional foods and sweets are made to Lord Khandoba. Prasad typically includes items like Puran Poli, Modak, and other festive delicacies.

Best Places to Celebrate:

Jejuri and Pali in Maharashtra are the main centers of Champa Shashthi celebrations, with grand processions and rituals held at the Khandoba temples.

Historical Significance:

The festival has deep historical roots in the region, highlighting the cultural and religious importance of Lord Khandoba in Maharashtra's heritage.

Activities:

Besides religious rituals, activities include cultural programs, folk dances, and community feasts. Devotees often participate in the traditional Lezim dance and Bhajan sessions.
