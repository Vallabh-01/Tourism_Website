Sabudana Khichdi

Description:

Sabudana Khichdi is a popular fasting dish made from soaked sabudana (tapioca pearls), peanuts, and mild spices, garnished with fresh coriander and lemon.

Ingredients:

Sabudana, peanuts, potatoes, green chilies, cumin seeds, coriander, lemon juice.

Preparation:

Sabudana is soaked overnight, then cooked with peanuts, potatoes, and spices until soft and flavorful. It is garnished with coriander and lemon juice before serving.

Best Places to Try:

Street food vendors and local eateries in Maharashtra, especially during fasting periods.

History:

Sabudana Khichdi is traditionally prepared during Hindu fasting periods like Navratri and Ekadashi, as it is easy to digest and provides sustained energy.

Nutritional Information:

Sabudana Khichdi is a high-carbohydrate dish, providing energy for fasting individuals. It also contains proteins from peanuts and essential vitamins from the added vegetables.

Variations:

Variations include adding grated coconut, ginger, and curry leaves for extra flavor. Some versions use ghee instead of oil for a richer taste.
