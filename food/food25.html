Kanda Bhaji

Description:

Kanda Bhaji, also known as Onion Pakoras, are crispy onion fritters made with gram flour, spices, and herbs.

Ingredients:

Onions, gram flour (besan), rice flour, chili powder, turmeric, ajwain (carom seeds), baking soda, salt, oil for frying.

Preparation:

Thinly sliced onions are mixed with gram flour, rice flour, spices, and herbs to form a thick batter. Small portions of the batter are deep-fried until golden brown and crispy.

Best Places to Try:

Kanda Bhaji is commonly found in street food stalls, tea shops, and snack centers across Maharashtra, especially during the monsoon season.

History:

Kanda Bhaji has been a popular snack in Maharashtra for generations, enjoyed for its crunchy texture and savory flavors.

Nutritional Information:

Kanda Bhaji is a deep-fried snack, rich in carbohydrates from gram flour and onions. It's best enjoyed in moderation as an occasional treat.

Variations:

Variations may include adding chopped spinach, fenugreek leaves, or other vegetables to the batter, or adjusting the spice level according to taste preferences.
