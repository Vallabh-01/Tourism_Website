Aashadhi Ekadashi

Description:

Aashadhi Ekadashi, also known as Devshayani Ekadashi, marks the beginning of the four-month period when Lord Vishnu is believed to be asleep. It is a significant day of fasting and prayers.

Significance:

The festival signifies the onset of the Chaturmas period, a time of penance and devotion in Hindu tradition.

Customs and Rituals:

Devotees observe a fast, engage in prayers and bhajans, and visit temples, especially the Vithoba temple in Pandharpur.

Special Foods:

Fasting foods like Sabudana Khichdi, fruits, and various traditional sweets are prepared and consumed during the day.

Best Places to Celebrate:

Pandharpur in Maharashtra is the epicenter of Aashadhi Ekadashi celebrations, attracting millions of devotees for the Wari pilgrimage.

Historical Significance:

The festival is deeply rooted in the Bhakti movement, emphasizing devotion and the loving worship of Lord Vithoba, a form of Vishnu.

Activities:

Besides fasting and prayers, devotees participate in the Dindi procession, singing abhangas (devotional songs) and engaging in communal worship.
