Pav Bhaji

Description:

A spicy vegetable mash served with buttery bread rolls (pav).

Ingredients:

Mixed vegetables (potatoes, tomatoes, peas, bell peppers), onions, garlic, ginger, pav bhaji masala, butter, pav bread.

Preparation:

The vegetables are boiled and mashed, then cooked with onions, garlic, ginger, and pav bhaji masala. It is served with buttered and toasted pav bread.

Best Places to Try:

Street vendors and restaurants in Mumbai, such as Sardar Pav Bhaji and Cannon Pav Bhaji.

History:

Pav Bhaji originated as a quick meal for Mumbai's textile mill workers in the 1850s. It has since become a popular street food across India.

Nutritional Information:

Pav Bhaji is rich in carbohydrates, fiber, and vitamins from the vegetables. However, it can be high in fat and calories due to the butter and pav bread.

Variations:

Variations include Cheese Pav Bhaji (topped with grated cheese), Jain Pav Bhaji (without onions and garlic), and Khada Pav Bhaji (with chunky vegetables).
