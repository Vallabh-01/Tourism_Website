Kojagiri Pournima

Description:

Kojagiri Pournima, also known as Sharad Purnima, is celebrated on the full moon night of the Hindu lunar month of Ashwin. It marks the end of the monsoon season and the harvest festival in Maharashtra, where people celebrate the abundance and prosperity brought by the harvest.

Significance:

The festival is dedicated to the moon and Goddess Lakshmi, the goddess of wealth and prosperity. It is believed that on this night, Goddess Lakshmi visits the homes of those who stay awake and blesses them with prosperity.

Customs and Rituals:

People perform special Lakshmi puja, stay awake all night, and sing devotional songs. It is also customary to prepare and consume sweetened milk, known as "Masala Doodh," under the moonlight.

Special Foods:

Masala Doodh (milk boiled with saffron, cardamom, and nuts), Kheer (rice pudding), and other sweet delicacies are prepared and enjoyed with family and friends.

Best Places to Celebrate:

The festival is widely celebrated across Maharashtra, but cities like Mumbai, Pune, and Nashik have large community gatherings and celebrations. Many people also visit nearby beaches and open spaces to enjoy the moonlight.

Historical Significance:

The festival has historical roots in the agrarian society of Maharashtra, symbolizing the end of the agricultural season and the time to enjoy the fruits of hard work. It also has mythological significance, associated with various legends of Goddess Lakshmi and Lord Krishna.

Activities:

Besides the religious rituals, activities include singing, dancing, and playing traditional games. People also participate in community gatherings, enjoy moonlit picnics, and spend time with family and friends.
