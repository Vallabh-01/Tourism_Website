Ghangad Fort

Famous For:

Ghangad Fort is known for its rugged terrain, dense forests, and panoramic views of the Mulshi Lake and surrounding hills. It holds historical significance as one of the forts in the Sahyadri mountain range and offers a glimpse into the region's rich history and natural beauty.

Activities to Do:

Visitors to Ghangad Fort can engage in trekking and hiking through the dense forests and rocky terrain. The fort's location amidst nature makes it an ideal spot for nature walks, bird watching, and photography. Adventure enthusiasts can explore the fort's caves and rock formations or indulge in rappelling and rock climbing activities.

Famous Places to Visit:

Some of the notable attractions within Ghangad Fort include the fortifications, bastions, and remnants of ancient structures. The scenic views of the Mulshi Lake and surrounding hills from the fort's vantage points are breathtaking. Visitors can also explore nearby attractions such as Sudhagad Fort and Tamhini Ghat.

How to Reach:

Ghangad Fort is located near Mulshi in the Pune district of Maharashtra. The base village for trekking to the fort is Ekole. Visitors can reach Ekole village by road from Pune or Mumbai. From Ekole, a trekking trail leads to the fort, which takes approximately 2-3 hours to complete. It is advisable to hire a local guide for the trek.

Best Time to Visit:

The best time to visit Ghangad Fort is during the monsoon season (June to September) when the surroundings are lush green, and the weather is cool and pleasant. The post-monsoon period (October to February) is also suitable for visiting, as the weather remains pleasant, and the surroundings are vibrant. It is advisable to avoid visiting during the summer months (March to May) due to hot weather.

Entry Fee:

There is no entry fee to visit Ghangad Fort. Visitors can explore the fort and its surroundings freely. However, it is essential to respect the natural environment and historical structures during the visit.

Accommodation:

Since Ghangad Fort is located in a remote area, there are limited accommodation options available nearby. Visitors can opt for camping near the fort or stay in homestays and guesthouses in nearby villages like Ekole and Mulshi. It is advisable to carry camping gear and other essentials for an overnight stay.
