Bharli Vangi

Description:

Bharli Vangi is a traditional Maharashtrian dish of stuffed eggplants cooked in a spicy and tangy gravy.

Ingredients:

Baby eggplants, peanuts, coconut, sesame seeds, tamarind, jaggery, spices (coriander, cumin, red chili), oil.

Preparation:

The eggplants are slit and stuffed with a mixture of ground peanuts, coconut, and spices, then cooked in a tangy gravy until tender.

Best Places to Try:

Local households and traditional Maharashtrian restaurants.

History:

Bharli Vangi has its roots in the rich culinary traditions of Maharashtra, often served during special occasions and festivals.

Nutritional Information:

This dish is rich in fiber, vitamins, and minerals from the eggplants and nuts used in the stuffing.

Variations:

Variations can include different stuffing ingredients such as onions, garlic, and other regional spices.
