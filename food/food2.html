Vada Pav

Description:

Often referred to as the Indian burger, it consists of a spicy potato fritter in a bun, served with chutneys.

Ingredients:

Potatoes, green chilies, garlic, ginger, spices, gram flour, pav bread, chutneys (tamarind, mint, and dry garlic).

Preparation:

The potato mixture is spiced and formed into balls, dipped in gram flour batter, and deep-fried. The vada is then placed in a pav with chutneys.

Best Places to Try:

Street vendors in Mumbai, such as Anand Stall and Ashok Vada Pav.

History:

Vada Pav was invented in Mumbai in the 1960s as a quick, affordable snack for the city's bustling workforce.

Nutritional Information:

Vada Pav is high in carbohydrates and fats due to the potatoes and deep-frying process. It also provides some protein from the gram flour.

Variations:

Variations include Cheese Vada Pav (with cheese slices), Schezwan Vada Pav (with spicy Schezwan sauce), and Jain Vada Pav (without onions and garlic).
