Vat Purnima

Description:

Vat Purnima is a festival celebrated by married women in Maharashtra, praying for the longevity and prosperity of their husbands.

Significance:

This festival is inspired by the legend of Savitri and Satyavan, symbolizing marital fidelity and devotion.

Customs and Rituals:

Married women fast and tie threads around a banyan tree, offering prayers and seeking blessings for their husbands' well-being.

Special Foods:

Special fasting foods and sweets are prepared, including Puran Poli, Sabudana Khichdi, and various fruits.

Best Places to Celebrate:

Vat Purnima is celebrated in households and temples across Maharashtra, with significant gatherings in Pune and Mumbai.

Historical Significance:

The festival's roots can be traced to the Mahabharata, highlighting the virtues of loyalty and devotion in marital relationships.

Activities:

Women participate in group prayers, storytelling sessions about Savitri and Satyavan, and community feasts.
