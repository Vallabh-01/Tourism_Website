Poha

Description:

Flattened rice cooked with onions, potatoes, and mild spices, often garnished with fresh coriander, sev, and lemon.

Ingredients:

Flattened rice (poha), onions, potatoes, green chilies, mustard seeds, turmeric, coriander, lemon, sev.

Preparation:

The flattened rice is rinsed and drained. Onions, potatoes, and spices are saut√©ed, then mixed with the poha and cooked until soft. It is garnished with coriander, sev, and lemon.

Best Places to Try:

Local eateries and street vendors in Maharashtra, especially in cities like Indore and Mumbai.

History:

Poha has been a staple breakfast in Maharashtra for generations. Its simplicity and quick preparation make it a popular choice.

Nutritional Information:

Poha is low in calories and fat, and provides a good amount of carbohydrates and fiber. The addition of peanuts or sev can increase the fat content.

Variations:

Variations include Kanda Poha (with onions), Batata Poha (with potatoes), and a combination of both. Some regions also add peas, peanuts, and grated coconut for extra flavor and texture.
