Batata Wada

Description:

Batata Wada is a popular street food snack made of spicy mashed potatoes, coated in gram flour batter and deep-fried.

Ingredients:

Potatoes, gram flour, green chilies, garlic, mustard seeds, turmeric, oil for frying.

Preparation:

Mashed potatoes are spiced with green chilies, garlic, mustard seeds, and turmeric, then shaped into balls, coated in gram flour batter, and deep-fried until golden brown.

Best Places to Try:

Street food stalls and local eateries across Maharashtra, especially in Mumbai.

History:

Batata Wada has been a popular street food in Maharashtra for decades, often enjoyed with pav (bread) or as a snack.

Nutritional Information:

Batata Wada is high in carbohydrates and fats due to the potatoes and deep-frying process. It provides a quick source of energy.

Variations:

Variations include adding different spices to the potato mixture or using a different type of batter.
