Thalipeeth

Description:

Thalipeeth is a multi-grain flatbread made from a mix of flours and spices, often served with butter or yogurt.

Ingredients:

Wheat flour, sorghum flour, millet flour, gram flour, spices, onions, coriander.

Preparation:

A mix of flours is combined with spices, onions, and coriander to form a dough. The dough is shaped into flatbreads and cooked on a griddle until crisp and golden.

Best Places to Try:

Local households and eateries in Maharashtra, often as a breakfast or snack item.

History:

Thalipeeth is a traditional Maharashtrian dish, often prepared in rural areas using locally available grains and flours.

Nutritional Information:

Thalipeeth is a nutritious flatbread, rich in fiber, proteins, and vitamins from the variety of grains used.

Variations:

Variations include adding different vegetables or greens to the dough, or using different combinations of flours.
