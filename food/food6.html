Bhakarwadi

Description:

Bhakarwadi is a crispy and spicy snack made from gram flour dough stuffed with a mix of coconut, poppy seeds, and spices, then rolled and fried.

Ingredients:

Gram flour, coconut, poppy seeds, sesame seeds, spices, oil for frying.

Preparation:

A dough is made from gram flour and rolled out. The stuffing of coconut, poppy seeds, and spices is spread on the dough, which is then rolled, sliced, and deep-fried until crispy.

Best Places to Try:

Chitale Bandhu Mithaiwale and other sweet shops in Pune and Mumbai.

History:

Bhakarwadi originated in the western Indian state of Maharashtra, particularly in the city of Pune. It has been a popular snack for decades.

Nutritional Information:

Bhakarwadi is high in calories due to the deep-frying process. It provides carbohydrates, fats, and a moderate amount of protein.

Variations:

Some variations include adding dried fruits or different spices to the filling. The sweetness and spiciness can be adjusted according to preference.
