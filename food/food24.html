Pithla Bhakri

Description:

Pithla Bhakri is a rustic dish from Maharashtra, consisting of spiced gram flour curry served with thick flatbread (bhakri).

Ingredients:

Gram flour (besan), onions, garlic, green chilies, turmeric, mustard seeds, cumin seeds, asafoetida, oil, water, salt.

Preparation:

Gram flour is mixed with water to form a smooth batter. Onions, garlic, and green chilies are saut√©ed with mustard seeds, cumin seeds, and turmeric. The gram flour batter is then added and cooked until thickened. The bhakri is made by kneading dough from millet or sorghum flour, shaping it into flatbreads, and cooking on a griddle.

Best Places to Try:

Pithla Bhakri is commonly enjoyed in rural areas of Maharashtra, especially during lunchtime in local households or roadside eateries.

History:

Pithla Bhakri has been a traditional Maharashtrian dish for centuries, known for its simplicity, affordability, and nourishment.

Nutritional Information:

Pithla Bhakri is a nutritious meal, rich in protein from gram flour and carbohydrates from bhakri, providing energy and sustenance.

Variations:

Variations of Pithla may include adding vegetables such as spinach or fenugreek leaves, or adjusting spices according to taste preferences.
