Ukadiche Modak

Description:

Ukadiche Modak is a traditional Maharashtrian sweet dumpling made from rice flour dough filled with a coconut and jaggery mixture.

Ingredients:

Rice flour, jaggery (or sugar), grated coconut, cardamom powder, ghee, salt.

Preparation:

The rice flour dough is prepared by mixing hot water, salt, and a little ghee, which is then shaped into small cups. A sweet mixture of grated coconut, jaggery, and cardamom powder is stuffed inside these cups and sealed. The modaks are then steamed until cooked.

Best Places to Try:

Ukadiche Modak is often made at home during the Ganesh Chaturthi festival in Maharashtra. It can also be found at sweet shops and during festive occasions.

History:

Ukadiche Modak has a special significance in Hindu mythology and is considered a favorite of Lord Ganesha, the remover of obstacles. It is often prepared as an offering during the Ganesh festival.

Nutritional Information:

Ukadiche Modak is rich in carbohydrates, proteins, and essential nutrients from rice flour, coconut, and jaggery. However, it is also high in calories and should be consumed in moderation.

Variations:

Variations of Ukadiche Modak may include different fillings such as dry fruits, nuts, or khoya (mawa), and the outer covering may be made from wheat flour instead of rice flour.
